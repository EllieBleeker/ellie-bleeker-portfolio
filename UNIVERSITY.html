University

During my time at Northumbria University, I tried to push myself to create unique designs that were eyecatching.

The ZaVi Chair

I created a chair exploring the Deconstructivist movement with elements inspired by the architecture of Zaha Hadid. The form of the chair was specifically influenced by the Vitra Fire Station. Throughout the design process I made desicisions based on continuous research, as well as feedback from peers and tutors.

Made from steel and sheepskin.
Designed, made and photographed by me.

Flatware

I designed a contemporary range of flatware (knife, fork and spoon) that took into consideration, historical and contextual issues, ergonomics, hygiene and function. The flatware was inspired by the human body. The fork was reminicent of a hand, the knife a leg and the spoon had the curves of a bottom.

Made from brass and silver plated.
Designed, made and photographed by me.

QuartaCirculo

Tasked with creating a product that utilises an extruded Aluminium component I designed a small circular table lamp. I designed and 3D printed an extrusion that acts as a heatsink for the lighting and created a wooden base that the extrusion slots into.
While focusing on the extrusion itself, I also explored how wood and metal can be joined without the need for extra screws or fixtures.

Made from oak and 3D printed "aluminium" extrusion.
Designed, made and photographed by me.
